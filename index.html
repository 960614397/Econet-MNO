Section 01

What is an MVNO?

A Mobile Virtual Network Operator (MVNO) is a brand that sells mobile services without owning the underlying radio network. It rides on a host MNO's coverage and competes on brand, customer experience, and the value layered on top of the SIM.

MNO Mobile Network Operator

Owns the towers, spectrum and core network. Provides wholesale capacity. In Zimbabwe, that's Econet.

That's us
MVNE Mobile Virtual Network Enabler

Operates the BSS, OCS, provisioning and day-to-day commercial running of the MVNO. This is us.

MVNO Mobile Virtual Network Operator

Owns the brand, the customer relationship and the go-to-market. The face of the business.

Econet Wireless Zimbabwe — the foundation every MVNO is built on.

Every MVNO we launch in Zimbabwe rides Econet's network. Econet provides the wholesale layer it already owns; DSG brings the brands, the platform and the day-to-day operations. The result is a new, high-margin revenue line on infrastructure that is already built — with the operational burden carried by us, not Econet.

Econet

The host MNO. Provides the network and the wholesale relationship.

DSG — MVNE

Runs the platform, billing, care, VAS and every MVNO on the network.

The brands

Retail, banking, faith, diaspora, agri, enterprise — each its own MVNO.

What Econet manages as the host MNO

Eight wholesale responsibilities — all within Econet's existing core competency. This is the network layer Econet already runs every day; the MVNO simply rides on top of it.

01

Radio access & coverage

National 2G / 3G / 4G LTE (and 5G where live) coverage. The MVNO rides Econet's RAN; capacity is allocated to MVNO traffic.

02

Core network & provisioning

HLR / HSS subscriber provisioning, IMSI activation and enablement of voice, SMS and data bearers on the core.

03

Numbering & SIM / eSIM identity

MSISDN ranges and IMSI allocation for the MVNO. SIM and eSIM profiles issued on Econet IMSIs.

04

Wholesale rate card & billing

Wholesale tariffs for voice, SMS and data. CDR feeds to the MVNE and monthly reconciliation & settlement.

05

Interconnect & roaming

National interconnect with other Zimbabwe operators, plus international roaming and IDD for MVNO subscribers.

06

Regulatory & compliance interface

POTRAZ licensing alignment, SIM registration, lawful intercept and number-portability obligations.

07

Quality of service & SLAs

Network availability, throughput and an agreed service-level commitment to the MVNO tier.

08

Capacity planning

Forecasting and dimensioning incremental MVNO load so it never degrades Econet's own subscriber base.

Econet provides the network. DSG carries everything else.

The whole point of an MVNE is that the host MNO does not take on retail operations. Econet stays focused on the network; DSG runs every customer-facing and back-office function for each MVNO.

How the model works — and how Econet earns

A clean three-party flow. The subscriber deals with the brand; DSG runs the operation; Econet earns a wholesale annuity on every SIM and every byte.

Layer 1

MVNO brand

Owns the customer, sets retail pricing, runs marketing & distribution. Collects subscriber revenue.

Layer 2

DSG — MVNE

Runs BSS/OCS, care, VAS and billing. Charges the MVNO a managed-service fee; settles wholesale with Econet.

Layer 3

Econet — host MNO

Provides wholesale network. Earns per-SIM access + per-unit usage, plus agreed minimum commitments.

Per-SIM access

A recurring wholesale charge for every active subscriber on the network.

Usage wholesale

Voice, SMS and data billed at the wholesale rate card as subscribers consume.

Minimum commitments

Take-or-pay floors underwrite a predictable baseline regardless of ramp.
